How does SecretShare work?

SecretShare lets you share sensitive information through one-time, self-destructing links. The flow is simple: you paste your secret, we encrypt it in your browser, and you get a unique link. Send that link to your recipient — when they open it, the secret is decrypted in their browser and permanently deleted from our servers. No one can view it again, not even you.

What encryption does SecretShare use?

SecretShare uses AES-256-GCM, a military-grade authenticated encryption algorithm, via the browser's built-in Web Crypto API. No third-party cryptography libraries are involved. Your secret is encrypted and decrypted entirely within your browser — the plaintext never touches our servers.

What does "zero-knowledge" mean?

When you create a secret, the encryption key is placed in the URL fragment — the part after the # symbol. Per RFC 3986, browsers never send the fragment to servers. This means our servers physically cannot see or log the decryption key. We store only the encrypted blob and have no way to read your secret — ever.

How does view-once burn work?

Each secret is stored in its own isolated Durable Object on Cloudflare's network. Durable Objects provide single-threaded execution, which means only one request can process at a time. When someone retrieves a secret, the encrypted data is atomically deleted before the response is sent. Even if two people click the link at the exact same instant, only one will ever see the secret — the other gets a "burned" message.

How does password protection work?

Password protection adds a second layer of encryption on top of the URL key. Your password is run through PBKDF2 with 600,000 iterations (per OWASP 2024 guidelines) to derive a strong encryption key. The secret is then double-encrypted: first with the URL key, then with the password-derived key. The recipient needs both the link and the password to decrypt. Think of it like two-factor authentication for your secret — share the link in one channel and the password in another.

What expiration options are available?

Secrets can be set to expire after 1 hour, 24 hours, or 7 days. Signed-in users also get a 30-day option. When a secret expires, the encrypted data is permanently wiped — only minimal metadata is kept briefly for status checks, then that is cleaned up too. Expired secrets cannot be recovered.

What are the best practices for sharing?

For maximum security, send the SecretShare link through an end-to-end encrypted channel like Signal, WhatsApp, or encrypted email. If you use password protection, send the password through a different channel than the link — for example, send the link via Slack and the password via text message. This way, compromising a single channel is not enough to access the secret.

Is SecretShare scalable and reliable?

SecretShare runs on Cloudflare's global edge network, spanning 300+ data centers worldwide. Each secret is an independent Durable Object — there's no shared database that could become a bottleneck or single point of failure. Whether one person or thousands create secrets at the same time, each one is handled independently at the edge location nearest to the user.

What can SecretShare's servers see?

Our servers store only the encrypted blob, a secret ID, and basic metadata (creation time, expiry, and status). We never have access to the encryption key, the plaintext, or any password you set. Even if our servers were completely compromised, an attacker would only find encrypted data that is computationally infeasible to decrypt without the key — which only exists in the URL you shared.
